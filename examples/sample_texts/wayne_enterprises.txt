Wayne Enterprises is one of the largest and most diversified privately held conglomerates in the United States, with its headquarters in Gotham City, New Jersey. The company was founded in 1855 and has remained under the stewardship of the Wayne family for over 165 years, currently led by Executive Chairman Bruce Wayne.

The conglomerate operates across seven distinct business divisions: Wayne Technologies (defense electronics, advanced materials research), Wayne Biotech (pharmaceutical R&D, medical devices), Wayne Shipping (global freight and port management), Wayne Financial (investment management, private lending), Wayne Real Estate (commercial and mixed-use development), Wayne Media (broadcast, digital content), and Wayne Foundation (philanthropic arm, not for profit).

Wayne Enterprises employs more than 80,000 people worldwide and operates in 42 countries. Revenue across all divisions has historically been reported in the range of $31 billion to $35 billion annually, making it one of the top 100 largest companies in the United States by revenue. The company is not publicly traded.

Wayne Technologies is widely regarded as the most strategically important division, with multi-billion dollar contracts with the U.S. Department of Defense for stealth communications systems, next-generation body armor composites, and autonomous reconnaissance drones.

The company has been active in M&A, completing 17 acquisitions in the past decade. Recent transactions include the acquisition of LexCorp's satellite communications unit, a joint venture with Ferris Aircraft for urban air mobility vehicles, and the spin-off of its legacy printing and publishing assets.

Wayne Enterprises is known for its long-term orientation, conservative balance sheet, and significant philanthropic commitments through the Wayne Foundation, which distributes over $300 million annually to urban development and education initiatives globally.
