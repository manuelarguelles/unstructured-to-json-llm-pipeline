Globex Industries is a diversified heavy manufacturing conglomerate with its global headquarters in Springfield, Ohio. Established in 1962, Globex has grown through a combination of organic expansion and strategic acquisitions to become one of the top 20 industrial manufacturers in North America by revenue.

The company operates across four primary business units: Advanced Materials (composites, specialty alloys), Industrial Automation (robotic assembly systems, PLC controllers), Energy Systems (turbine components, heat exchangers), and Defense Contracts (precision-machined components for aerospace and military applications).

Globex currently employs over 18,500 people across 34 manufacturing plants in the United States, Mexico, Germany, and South Korea. Annual revenues for the most recent fiscal year were reported at approximately $3.2 billion, with EBITDA margins of roughly 14%.

The company's Advanced Materials division has seen the strongest growth in recent years, driven by surging demand from the electric vehicle sector for lightweight composite components. Globex recently signed a 5-year supply agreement with two major EV manufacturers in the United States and Europe.

Key products include the GX-900 series robotic welding arms, Thermalloy-X structural composites, and the PowerCore turbine blade system. The company holds 142 active patents across its product lines.

Despite macro headwinds in the broader industrial sector, Globex has maintained its investment-grade credit rating and continues to pay a quarterly dividend. The board recently approved a $500 million capital expenditure program to modernize aging production lines and expand capacity in the Advanced Materials segment.
